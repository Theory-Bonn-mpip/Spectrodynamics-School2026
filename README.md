## School Format and Philosophy

Over the past years, a wide range of high-quality lecture material on computational spectroscopy has become freely available online. While these resources are extremely valuable, they cannot fully replace **direct interaction, hands-on problem solving, and in-depth scientific discussion**.

For this reason, the SpectroDynamics school is designed not as a traditional conference or lecture-only event, but as a **cohesive training school** that combines:

- foundational and advanced lectures,
- **three extended hands-on tutorial sessions**, and
- ample time for discussion, interaction, and collaboration.

A key component of the school is the practical sessions, where participants will work closely with instructors, tutors, and fellow students to implement and apply computational spectroscopy methods. These sessions are intended to help participants **translate theoretical knowledge into tools they can use in their own research**.

Participation in the school is **application-based**, and selection will be made to ensure a strong match between the program and the participants‚Äô backgrounds and interests.

A **strong application** should clearly demonstrate:
